Special Appointing Authorities for Veterans

Beyond Veterans' Preference, several special appointing authorities exist to help veterans gain federal employment. These authorities allow agencies to hire eligible veterans non-competitively or with streamlined processes.

Key Appointing Authorities:

  • Veterans Recruitment Appointment (VRA): Allows agencies to appoint eligible veterans without competition up to the GS-11 level (or equivalent). Veterans must meet specific service requirements and be honorably discharged.
  • 30% or More Disabled Veteran: Permits agencies to non-competitively appoint veterans with a 30% or more service-connected disability. There is no grade-level restriction for this authority.
  • Disabled Veterans Enrolled in a VA Training Program: Allows agencies to appoint veterans who are receiving training under the VA's vocational rehabilitation program.
  • Veterans Employment Opportunity Act (VEOA): Provides eligible veterans with the opportunity to compete for positions announced under agency merit promotion procedures when the agency is recruiting from outside its own workforce.

Benefits of Special Authorities:

  • Streamlined Hiring: Reduces the time and complexity of the hiring process for both agencies and veterans.
  • Increased Opportunities: Opens up additional pathways to federal service for eligible veterans.
  • Targeted Recruitment: Enables agencies to specifically target and hire veterans for various roles.

It is important for veterans to understand which appointing authorities they may be eligible for and to clearly state their eligibility when applying for federal jobs. For comprehensive details, refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - Special Appointing Authorities Section.
