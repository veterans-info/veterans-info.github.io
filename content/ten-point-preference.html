10-Point Preference (CP, CPS, XP)

Ten-point preference is granted to veterans with service-connected disabilities, Purple Heart recipients, and certain spouses, widows, or mothers of veterans. This preference adds ten points to their earned score on federal civil service examinations.

Types of 10-Point Preference:

  • CP (Compensable Disability, 10 Points): For veterans who have a compensable service-connected disability of 10% or more.
  • CPS (Compensable Disability, Spousal Preference, 10 Points): For spouses of veterans who are unable to work due to a service-connected disability, or spouses of deceased veterans who died of a service-connected disability.
  • XP (Non-Compensable Disability or Purple Heart, 10 Points): For veterans with a non-compensable service-connected disability or those who received a Purple Heart.

Key Eligibility Criteria:

  • Service-Connected Disability: A disability rating from the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) is required for CP and CPS.
  • Purple Heart: Receipt of the Purple Heart medal.
  • Relationship to Veteran: For derivative preference (CPS), proof of relationship and the veteran's disability or death is necessary.

Documentation required typically includes a DD Form 214 and a VA letter confirming disability rating or other relevant documentation. For comprehensive details on 10-point preference categories, refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - 10-Point Preference Section.
