Eligibility Overview

Veterans' preference in federal employment is generally based on honorable active duty service in the U.S. Armed Forces. Eligibility criteria vary depending on the type of preference sought (5-point or 10-point).

General Requirements:

  • Active Duty Service: Generally, preference applies to veterans who served on active duty in the U.S. Armed Forces.
  • Honorable Discharge: Service must have been performed under honorable conditions.
  • Specific Dates/Campaigns: Eligibility often depends on the dates of service or participation in specific military campaigns or expeditions.

Key Eligibility Categories:

The OPM Vet Guide outlines several categories of veterans who may be eligible for preference, including:

  • Veterans who served during a war.
  • Veterans who served in a campaign or expedition for which a campaign badge has been authorized.
  • Veterans who served for more than 180 consecutive days, any part of which occurred after January 31, 1955, and before October 15, 1976.
  • Veterans who served in a declared hostile environment.
  • Disabled veterans (those with a service-connected disability).

For detailed information on specific eligibility criteria and exceptions, please refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - Eligibility Section.
