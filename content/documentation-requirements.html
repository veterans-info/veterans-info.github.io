Documentation Requirements for Veterans' Preference

To claim Veterans' Preference in federal employment, applicants must provide specific documentation to verify their eligibility. The required documents vary depending on the type of preference being claimed.

Commonly Required Documents:

  • DD Form 214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty): This is the primary document for verifying active duty service and character of discharge. It is essential for all preference claims.
  • SF-15 (Application for 10-Point Veteran Preference): Required for all 10-point preference claims. This form helps determine the specific type of 10-point preference and guides the applicant on additional required documentation.
  • VA Disability Letter: For veterans claiming 10-point preference based on a service-connected disability, a letter from the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) confirming the disability rating is necessary.
  • Marriage Certificate/Death Certificate: For individuals claiming derivative preference (e.g., spouses or widows/widowers), proof of relationship to the veteran and, if applicable, the veteran's death certificate are required.

Key Considerations:

  • Provide All Required Documents: Failure to submit all necessary documentation by the application deadline can result in the denial of preference.
  • Legible Copies: Ensure all submitted documents are clear and legible.
  • Specific Instructions: Always follow the specific documentation instructions provided in the job announcement.

It is highly recommended to have all your military and VA documents organized and readily available when applying for federal jobs. For comprehensive details on documentation requirements, refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - Documentation Section.
