5-Point Preference (TP)

5-point preference adds five points to the passing examination score of eligible veterans. This is the most common type of Veterans' Preference for non-disabled veterans who served during specific periods or campaigns.

Who Qualifies for 5-Point Preference?

Five points are added to the passing examination score or rating of a veteran who served:

During a War

  • World War II (December 7, 1941 through April 28, 1952)
  • Note: For federal employment purposes, only wars declared by Congress qualify

Specific Time Periods

  • Korean War Era: April 28, 1952 through July 1, 1955
  • Vietnam Era: More than 180 consecutive days, any part between January 31, 1955 and October 15, 1976 (for other than training)
  • Gulf War: August 2, 1990 through January 2, 1992
  • Post-9/11: More than 180 consecutive days, any part between September 11, 2001 and August 31, 2010 (Operation Iraqi Freedom end date)

Campaign or Expedition Medal Recipients

Veterans who served in a campaign or expedition for which a campaign medal has been authorized, including:

  • Armed Forces Expeditionary Medal
  • Southwest Asia Service Medal
  • Kosovo Campaign Medal
  • Afghanistan Campaign Medal
  • Iraq Campaign Medal
  • Inherent Resolve Campaign Medal
  • And other authorized campaign medals

Service Requirements

180-Day Rule for Recent Veterans

Veterans who enlisted after September 7, 1980, or entered active duty after October 14, 1982, must have:

  • Served continuously for 24 months OR
  • Completed the full period for which called or ordered to active duty

Note: This requirement does not apply to veterans separated for disability or those who served during the Gulf War period.

How 5-Point Preference Works

In Competitive Examinations

When agencies use numerical scoring:

  • 5 points are added to your passing score
  • Example: If you score 85 on an exam, your final score is 90
  • You must first achieve a passing score (usually 70) to receive the points

In Category Rating

When agencies use category rating:

  • Preference eligibles are listed ahead of non-preference eligibles in each quality category
  • Points are not added, but preference still provides an advantage

In Reduction in Force (RIF)

5-point preference eligibles receive:

  • Higher retention standing than non-veterans
  • Placement in retention subgroup A

Required Documentation

To claim 5-point preference, you must provide:

  • DD Form 214 showing:
    • Honorable or general discharge
    • Dates of service qualifying for preference OR
    • Campaign/expeditionary medal
  • No SF-15 required (unlike 10-point preference)

Common Scenarios

Gulf War Veteran

Served on active duty from June 1990 to June 1994:

  • ✓ Qualifies for 5-point preference (served during Gulf War period)
  • Needs: DD-214 showing honorable discharge and service dates

Afghanistan Campaign Medal Recipient

Served in Afghanistan and received campaign medal:

  • ✓ Qualifies for 5-point preference (campaign medal recipient)
  • Needs: DD-214 showing medal and honorable discharge

Peacetime Service Only

Served 1977-1980 with no campaign medals:

  • ✗ Does not qualify for 5-point preference
  • No qualifying war, campaign, or time period

Important Notes

Key Points to Remember

  • 5-point preference is for non-disabled veterans only
  • Veterans with service-connected disabilities may qualify for 10-point preference instead
  • You cannot receive both 5-point and 10-point preference
  • Active duty for training doesn't qualify unless you have a service-connected disability
  • National Guard and Reserve service must be active duty (not training) to qualify

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I served during peacetime but was deployed to Bosnia. Do I qualify?

A: If you received a campaign or expeditionary medal for your Bosnia service, you qualify for 5-point preference regardless of when you served.

Q: Does my Reserve service count?

A: Only if you were on active duty (not active duty for training) during a qualifying period or received a campaign medal.

Q: I have a 0% VA disability rating. Which preference do I claim?

A: With any service-connected disability rating from VA, you may be eligible for 10-point preference (XP category) instead of 5-point.
