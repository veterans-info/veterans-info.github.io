5-Point Preference (TP)

Five-point preference (TP) is granted to veterans who served on active duty in the U.S. Armed Forces during certain periods or in specific campaigns, and who were honorably discharged. This preference adds five points to their earned score on federal civil service examinations.

Eligibility Criteria for 5-Point Preference:

  • Service During a War: Served during a war, or in a campaign or expedition for which a campaign badge has been authorized.
  • Service for More Than 180 Consecutive Days: Served for more than 180 consecutive days, any part of which occurred after January 31, 1955, and before October 15, 1976.
  • Honorable Discharge: Received an honorable discharge from service.

Key Points:

  • This preference is typically for veterans without a service-connected disability.
  • It applies to competitive examinations for federal jobs.
  • Documentation required usually includes a DD Form 214, Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty.

For detailed information on 5-point preference, refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - 5-Point Preference Section.
