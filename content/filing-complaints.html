Filing Complaints Regarding Veterans' Preference

If a veteran believes that an agency has violated their Veterans' Preference rights, they have avenues to file a complaint and seek redress. It is important to understand the proper procedures and timelines for filing such complaints.

Where to File a Complaint:

  • Department of Labor (DOL) - Veterans' Employment and Training Service (VETS): For violations of Veterans' Preference in the hiring process. VETS investigates complaints and attempts to resolve them.
  • Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB): For certain adverse actions (e.g., removal, suspension for more than 14 days, reduction in grade or pay) where Veterans' Preference rights may have been violated.
  • Office of Special Counsel (OSC): For prohibited personnel practices, which may include violations of Veterans' Preference.

Key Steps in Filing a Complaint:

  • Gather Documentation: Collect all relevant documents, including job announcements, application materials, correspondence with the agency, and any evidence of the alleged violation.
  • Understand Timelines: Be aware of the strict deadlines for filing complaints with each body (e.g., 60 days for VETS complaints).
  • Seek Guidance: Consider consulting with a veterans' service organization or legal counsel specializing in federal employment law.

For detailed information on filing complaints and the specific procedures for each body, refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - Filing Complaints Section.
