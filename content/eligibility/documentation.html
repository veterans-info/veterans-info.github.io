Required Documentation for Veterans' Preference

To claim Veterans' Preference, you must provide specific documentation that proves your eligibility. The documents required depend on your type of preference and individual circumstances.

Essential Documents for All Veterans

DD Form 214

The Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty (DD-214) is the most important document for proving Veterans' Preference eligibility. It shows:

  • Dates of active duty service
  • Character of discharge
  • Campaign medals and awards
  • Type of separation

Important

Member Copy 4 of the DD-214 is typically required as it contains all necessary information including character of discharge.

Alternative Documents

If you don't have your DD-214, you may submit:

  • Statement of Service - Letter from your branch of service
  • Military Service Records - From the National Archives
  • Discharge Certificate - If issued by your service branch
  • NGB Form 22 - For National Guard members

Documents by Preference Type

5-Point Preference (TP)

Required documents:

  • DD Form 214 showing:
    • Honorable or general discharge, AND
    • Active duty service during qualifying period OR
    • Campaign medal or expeditionary medal

10-Point Compensable Disability Preference (CP/CPS)

Required documents:

  • DD Form 214
  • VA Letter showing:
    • Service-connected disability rating
    • Percentage of disability (10% or more for CP, 30% or more for CPS)
  • SF-15 (Application for 10-Point Veteran Preference)

10-Point Disability Preference (XP)

Required documents vary by situation:

Purple Heart Recipients
  • DD Form 214 showing Purple Heart award
  • SF-15
Service-Connected Disability (less than 10%)
  • DD Form 214
  • VA letter showing service-connected disability
  • SF-15

10-Point Derived Preference (XP)

Required documents for spouses, widows/widowers, and mothers:

  • Documentation proving relationship to veteran:
    • Marriage certificate
    • Birth certificate (for mothers)
    • Death certificate (if applicable)
  • Veteran's DD Form 214
  • VA letter confirming veteran's disability or death status
  • SF-15
  • Additional documents depending on specific situation

Special Documentation Situations

Active Duty Service Members (VOW Act)

Under the VOW Act, active duty members within 120 days of discharge may submit:

  • Certification Letter on military letterhead containing:
    • Expected discharge date (within 120 days)
    • Character of expected discharge
    • Military service dates
  • DD-214 must be provided before appointment

Campaign Medal Recipients

Veterans claiming preference based on campaign or expeditionary medals must provide:

  • DD Form 214 showing the specific medal
  • OR military orders and award certificate if DD-214 doesn't list the medal

How to Obtain Documents

DD Form 214

  • Online: National Archives website
  • Mail: Submit SF-180 to National Personnel Records Center
  • In Person: Visit your local VA office

VA Disability Letter

  • Online: Through VA.gov account
  • Phone: Call 1-800-827-1000
  • In Person: Visit your local VA Regional Office

SF-15 Form

  • Download from OPM website
  • Available from agency HR offices
  • Must be completed and signed

Document Requirements

All documents must be:

  • Legible - Clear enough to read all information
  • Complete - Show all required information
  • Unaltered - No changes or modifications
  • Valid - Current for disability ratings

Tips for Document Submission

  • Submit copies - Never send original documents unless specifically required
  • Check requirements - Each job announcement may have specific instructions
  • Verify completeness - Ensure all pages are included
  • Keep records - Maintain copies of everything you submit
  • Update as needed - Keep disability ratings current

Common Documentation Mistakes

Avoid these common errors:

  • Submitting Member Copy 2 instead of Member Copy 4 of DD-214
  • Using outdated VA disability letters
  • Forgetting to complete and sign SF-15
  • Not including all pages of multi-page documents
  • Submitting illegible copies

Source Information
