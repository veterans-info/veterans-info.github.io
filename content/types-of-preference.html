Types of Preference

Veterans' Preference is categorized into different types based on the nature of service and any service-connected disabilities. The primary types are 5-point and 10-point preference.

5-Point Preference (TP)

  • Generally granted to veterans who served during certain periods or in certain campaigns, but do not have a service-connected disability.
  • Requires honorable discharge.

10-Point Preference

This is further divided into several sub-categories:

  • 10-Point Compensable Disability (CP): For veterans who have a service-connected disability of 10% or more.
  • 10-Point Compensable Disability, Schedular (CPS): For veterans who have a service-connected disability of 30% or more.
  • 10-Point Other (XP): For veterans who are Purple Heart recipients or have certain other specific service-connected conditions.
  • 10-Point Derived Preference (SSP): For spouses, mothers, or guardians of veterans who are unable to use their preference due to disability or death.

For detailed criteria and specific requirements for each type of preference, please consult the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals.
