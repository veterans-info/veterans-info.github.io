Basic Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for Veterans' Preference in federal employment, individuals must meet specific criteria related to their military service. These fundamental requirements form the basis for all preference claims.

General Conditions for Preference:

  • Honorable Discharge: Service must have been performed under honorable conditions, as evidenced by a Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty (DD Form 214).
  • Active Duty Service: Generally, preference applies to veterans who served on active duty in the U.S. Armed Forces. This typically excludes service in the National Guard or Reserves unless it was for a period of active duty for training or for a period of active duty for other than training.
  • Specific Service Periods or Campaigns: Eligibility often depends on the dates of service or participation in specific military campaigns or expeditions. For example, service during a war, or in a campaign or expedition for which a campaign badge has been authorized.

Key Points to Consider:

  • Length of Service: For some preference categories, a minimum length of active duty service is required.
  • Disability Status: Veterans with service-connected disabilities may be eligible for different types of preference (e.g., 10-point preference).
  • Receipt of Campaign Badge: Service in certain campaigns or expeditions, even for short durations, can confer preference eligibility if a campaign badge has been awarded.

It is crucial to review your DD Form 214 and any other relevant military documents to confirm your eligibility. For comprehensive details, always refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - Eligibility Section.
