Veterans' Preference in Reductions in Force (RIF)

Veterans' preference plays a significant role in protecting federal employees during a Reduction in Force (RIF). While preference does not guarantee retention, it provides advantages that can help veterans retain their positions or secure new ones.

How Preference Applies in RIF:

  • Retention Standing: Veterans generally receive a higher retention standing than non-veterans with the same length of service and performance ratings. This means they are among the last to be affected by a RIF.
  • Order of Retention: Employees are grouped into retention registers based on their competitive level, service computation date, and veterans' preference status. Within each group, employees are ranked by their retention standing.
  • Preference for Disabled Veterans: Disabled veterans (those with a compensable service-connected disability of 30% or more) receive additional protection and are typically placed in a higher retention subgroup.

Key Considerations:

  • Waiver of Preference: In some cases, a veteran may waive their preference in a RIF, but this is generally not advisable.
  • RIF Avoidance Strategies: Agencies often explore RIF avoidance strategies before implementing a RIF, such as voluntary early retirement or separation incentives.
  • Appeals: Employees affected by a RIF have appeal rights to the Merit Systems Protection Board (MSPB).

Understanding your rights and the specific rules governing RIF is crucial. For comprehensive details, refer to the OPM Vet Guide for HR Professionals - RIF Section.
